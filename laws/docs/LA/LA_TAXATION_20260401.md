# Taxation Act

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Octal Cap**: Maximum tax rate of one-eighth (1/8) or 12.5%.  
- **Destitution Clause**: Prohibition on any tax that deprives a median-income individual of basic liberty or renders them destitute.

---

## Preamble
The Hudson Republic is founded on the principle that taxation shall serve only to support legitimate local governance and national sovereignty, without unduly burdening individual liberty or economic freedom. The federal level imposes no direct taxes on natural persons. All taxation authority is devolved to the Ridings, subject to strict constitutional limits designed to prevent destitution, coercion, or extractive abuse. Federal taxation is limited to specific, enumerated activities that undermine Republic sovereignty or economic self-reliance. All taxes, fees, fines, penalties, and other financial obligations shall be paid in full exclusively in physical bullion in accordance with the **[Legal Tender Act](../CA/CA_LEGALTENDER_20260401.md)** and the **[Banking and Reserves Act](../CA/CA_BANKANDRESERVE_20260401.md)**. No fiat currency, digital tokens, or other forms of payment shall be accepted.

---

## Section 1 — General Principles
**1.1 Prohibition of Direct Federal Tax**: No federal income tax, wealth tax, capital gains tax, or payroll tax shall be imposed on natural persons (citizens or residents).  
**1.2 Riding Exclusivity**: Ridings possess exclusive authority to impose local taxes (property, sales, excise), subject to the limits of this Act.  
**1.3 Transactional Basis**: Taxation must be transaction-based (voluntary exchange) rather than extractive (mere existence or accumulated wealth).  
**1.4 Destitution Clause**: No tax may deprive a median-income individual of basic liberty or render them destitute. Violations are unconstitutional and subject to immediate judicial challenge.

---

## Section 2 — Limits on Riding Taxation
**2.1 Maximum Rate (The Octal Cap)**: No sales, property, or excise tax imposed by a Riding may exceed **one-eighth (1/8)** of the value, equivalent to a maximum of **12.5%**.  
**2.2 Ceiling**: Ridings may set rates below this maximum but never above.  
**2.3 Breach**: Any attempt to exceed this limit is *void ab initio* and constitutes a breach of fiduciary duty by Riding officials.

---

## Section 3 — Federal Taxation (Enumerated)
**3.1 Enumerated Categories**: Federal taxation is strictly limited to:  
* Tariffs on international imports and exports.  
* Flat fees for federal transactional services (passports, registry filings).  
* Fines and penalties for violations of federal law.  

**3.2 Domestic Prohibition**: No federal tax may be imposed on domestic transactions, personal income, or property within the Republic.

---

## Section 4 — Tax Payable Period and Compliance
**4.1 Filing Deadline**: All tax filings and documentation must be submitted by **May 31** of the year following the tax year.  
**4.2 Payment Deadline**: All federal taxes must be paid in full by **June 30** of the filing year.  
**4.3 Late Payment Penalties**:  
* **Natural Persons**: One (1) troy ounce of silver per day.  
* **Registered Businesses**: One (1) troy ounce of gold per day.  
* Penalties accrue daily starting July 1.  

**4.4 Enforcement**: Unpaid liabilities remaining after **December 31** of the filing year may trigger:  
* Garnishment of wages or remuneration.  
* Immediate suspension or revocation of business licenses.  
* Criminal proceedings for willful non-compliance.

---

## Section 5 — Judicial Review
**5.1 Challenges**: Any tax measure violating the principles of this Act (including the Section 1.4 destitution prohibition) may be challenged via a **Legislative Challenge (LC)**. The **Superior Court** holds original jurisdiction, with the **Supreme Court** holding final appellate jurisdiction.
