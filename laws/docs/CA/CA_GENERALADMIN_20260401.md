# General Administration Act
**(Applies to All Government Institutions)**

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Age of Majority**: Nineteen (19) years.  
- **Language of Majority**: English (the sole official administrative and working language of the Hudson Republic).  
- **Language of Commerce**: English (the language of all international trade, contracts, and external documentation).  
- **Termination**: Immediate and permanent cessation of all duties, responsibilities, and authorities, resulting in full revocation of tenure or employment.  
- **On-boarding**: The formal process of hiring, security clearance, training, and oath administration for any public position.  
- **Hudson Intelligence Service (HIS)**: The unified national agency responsible for intelligence, policing, border security, and protective functions.

---

## Preamble
This Act establishes uniform, mandatory standards for eligibility, security, record-keeping, language administration, and operational integrity across every public office and government institution in the Hudson Republic. Its purpose is to guarantee competence, national security, fiduciary responsibility, and efficient governance at all levels while eliminating any possibility of foreign influence, corruption, or administrative capture. All financial transactions, fees, licensing, and settlements within government institutions shall be conducted exclusively in physical bullion in accordance with the **[Legal Tender Act](./CA_LEGALTENDER_20260401.md)**.

---

## Section 1 — Basic Requirements for All Public Positions
**1.1** All applicants for any government position (elected, appointed, or employed) must satisfy the following requirements prior to on-boarding:  
* Have reached the **Age of Majority** (19 years).  
* Hold **sole citizenship** in the Hudson Republic.  
* Demonstrate proficiency in the **Language of Majority** at or above GED-equivalent level in both spoken and written forms.  
* Pass, at their own expense, a standard criminal background check (including Interpol verification) and a credit bureau check.  

**1.2** All government records, trade documentation, receipts, and transcripts shall be maintained exclusively in the Language of Majority.  
**1.3** All archived records shall be retained for a minimum of ten (10) years in both secure physical and encrypted digital formats.

---

## Section 2 — Security Clearances
**2.1** Every public position requires an appropriate security clearance issued by the **Hudson Intelligence Service (HIS)**. Clearances are tiered as follows:  
* **Normal Clearance**: No access to sensitive information (e.g., clerks, administrative support).  
* **Enhanced Clearance**: Access to non-classified but operationally sensitive information.  
* **Top Clearance**: Positions involving classified national security, intelligence, or high-level decision-making.  

**2.2** Clearances are subject to periodic reinvestigation by the HIS.

---

## Section 3 — Language Administration
**3.1** The sole official administrative and working language is known as the **Language of Majority** or **Language of Commerce**.  
**3.2** All official government communications, legislation, and judicial proceedings shall be conducted and recorded in **Language of Majority**.  
**3.3 Official Submissions**: All complaints, petitions, or applications to government offices must be submitted in **Language of Majority**. Submissions in other languages require a certified translation; the **Language of Majority** version is deemed legally authoritative.  
**3.4 Translations for Courtesy**: Documents may be translated into other languages for convenience or accessibility. These translations are **not** legally authoritative or binding.  
**3.5 Additional Recognition**: Recognizing additional languages for specific administrative purposes requires formal legislative approval and shall not diminish the primacy of the **Language of Majority**.  
**3.6 Indigenous Rights**: Appropriate accommodations shall be made for Indigenous languages consistent with the supremacy of individual rights under **[The Individual (Sovereign) Act](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**.  
**3.7 Judicial Interpreters**: Courts may permit interpreters for fair hearings, but official records and judgments remain in **Language of Majority**.  
**3.8 Enforcement**: Attempting to enforce a language other than **Language of Majority** in mandated administrative functions constitutes an administrative irregularity subject to correction.

---

## Section 4 — General Provisions
**4.1** All security clearances and associated licensing fees are non-refundable.  
**4.2 Breaches**: Violation of clearance protocols or unauthorized disclosure of protected information constitutes a material breach of fiduciary duty.  
* **Consequences**: Immediate termination, permanent revocation of clearance, and criminal prosecution under the **Sequential Mandate** (cumulative sentencing up to life imprisonment).  
**4.3 Verification**: On-boarding for all positions includes final verification of compliance with this Act, the **[Elected Officials and Integrity Act](./CA_ELECTEDOFFICIALSINTEGRITY_20260401.md)**, and the **[Elections Act](./CA_ELECTIONS_20260401.md)**.
