# Jurisdiction Act

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Federal Supremacy**: The principle that federal legislative instruments (CA, LA, LB, EL, EB) prevail over Riding-level instruments in matters of enumerated federal jurisdiction.  
- **Enumerated Jurisdiction**: The specific, limited list of authorities granted to the federal government (Monetary Integrity, National Defence, Border Security, and Federal Courts).  
- **Residual Powers**: All authorities, responsibilities, and legislative domains not explicitly granted to the federal government, which are reserved to the Ridings or the People.  
- **Inter-Riding Dispute**: A legal or administrative conflict arising between two or more Ridings that cannot be resolved through local arbitration.  
- **Legislative Challenge (LC)**: A formal procedural mechanism to contest the constitutionality or jurisdictional validity of an enacted instrument.  
- **Language of Majority**: English, as defined in **[General Administration Act](./CA_GENERALADMIN_20260401.md)**, serving as the sole official administrative and working language of the federal government.

---

## Preamble
This Act defines the boundaries of authority within the Hudson Republic, establishing a strict binary between federal enumerated powers and local residual powers. It reinforces the modular nature of the Republic by prohibiting intermediate layers of government and ensuring that the Language of Majority remains the functional standard of federal administration. This linguistic standardization is established purely as a critical cost-cutting measure to prevent the fiscal burden of maintaining parallel federal administrative sets. This Act explicitly protects the cultural and linguistic autonomy of the Ridings, where all non-federal language laws and cultural matters are determined.

---

## Section 1 — Federal Enumerated Jurisdiction
**1.1** The federal government’s authority is strictly limited to the following domains:  
* **Monetary Integrity**: Management of the Hudson Ledger, bullion standards, and banking reserves.  
* **National Defence**: Oversight of the Hudson United Guard (HUG) and external security.  
* **Borders and Ports**: Administration of the Hudson Port Authority (HPA) and Hudson Border Control (HBC).  
* **Federal Judiciary**: Operation of the Superior and Supreme Courts for constitutional and inter-Riding matters.  

**1.2** Pursuant to the **[General Administration Act](./CA_GENERALADMIN_20260401.md)**, the Language of Majority is the sole official language for federal taxation, tariffs, and the resolution of federal legal disputes. This is a functional requirement for administrative efficiency and cryptographic auditability; it does not constitute a mandate for cultural erasure.

---

## Section 2 — Riding Residual Jurisdiction
**2.1** All powers not expressly delegated to the federal government in Section 1.1 are reserved exclusively to the Ridings and their respective Riding Boards.  
**2.2** These powers include, but are not limited to: housing, healthcare, education, welfare, local infrastructure, and land use.  
**2.3** Ridings possess the exclusive authority to determine local language laws, cultural preservation acts, and secondary administrative languages. The Republic recognizes the preservation of French and other heritage languages as a core responsibility of the Riding, not the Federal government.

---

## Section 3 — The Modularity Principle
**3.1** No provincial, state, or regional layer of government shall exist between the Riding and the Federal government.  
**3.2** Any attempt to create an intermediate administrative body constitutes a breach of the constitutional framework.  
**3.3** Coordination between Ridings for logistics or infrastructure (e.g., watershed management) shall be handled via inter-Riding agreements, not new government tiers.

---

## Section 4 — Conflict of Laws
**4.1** In matters of enumerated federal jurisdiction, federal law maintains supremacy.  
**4.2** In matters of residual jurisdiction, Riding law is supreme.  
**4.3** In any conflict between a Riding bylaw and **[The Individual (Sovereign) Act](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**, the rights of the Individual shall prevail absolutely.  
**4.4** If a federal instrument attempts to legislate within a residual domain (e.g., education), it is *ultra vires* and subject to immediate nullification via a Legislative Challenge.

---

## Section EX1 (Illustrative Examples)
**EX1.1** A Riding in a traditionally Francophone area passes a law requiring all local business signs to be in French. This is a valid exercise of residual power under Section 2.3.  
**EX1.2** The Federal government refuses to provide a secondary set of tax forms or a parallel bureaucracy in French. This is a valid exercise of Section 1.2 and the **[General Administration Act](./CA_GENERALADMIN_20260401.md)** intended to minimize federal overhead.  
**EX1.3** A citizen files a federal lawsuit regarding a border tariff. Per the Language of Majority mandate, the proceedings and records must be in **Language of Majority** for administrative efficiency, though the citizen may utilize an interpreter at their own expense or as provided by local Riding support.
