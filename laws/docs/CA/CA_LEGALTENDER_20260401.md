## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Bullion**: Physical metal in coin, round, or bar form composed of gold, silver, or copper meeting the purity and weight standards established in this Act and the attached Schedule.  
- **Founders Edition**: The official reference series of Hudson Republic bullion coins minted to establish initial circulation standards, security features, and public recognition.  
- **XRF assay**: X-ray fluorescence or equivalent non-destructive chemical analysis used to verify elemental composition and purity.  
- **Bring Your Own Bullion (BYOB)**: The program allowing physical bullion meeting the standards herein, regardless of origin or mint, to be accepted as legal tender.

## Preamble
This Act establishes the standards for all bullion accepted as legal tender within the Hudson Republic. Legal tender status is determined solely by verifiable physical properties — purity, weight, and integrity — and not by origin, minting authority, or markings. The Act distinguishes between commercial and institutional use, incorporates the official Bullion Denominations Schedule, and mandates verification to ensure integrity and trust in the monetary system.

---

## Section 1 — Acceptable Bullion for Merchants (Commercial Use)
Bullion qualifies as legal tender for everyday commerce if it fully complies with the purity, weight, and condition requirements set out in the **SCH_BULLIONDENOMINATIONS_20260504** Schedule.

---

## Section 2 — Acceptable Bullion for Financial Institutions
Bullion qualifies for use in banking, trusts, savings, and institutional reserves if it meets the higher purity and form standards set out in the **SCH_BULLIONDENOMINATIONS_20260504** Schedule. Institutional reserves shall be backed exclusively by bars meeting these standards.

---

## Section 3 — Bring Your Own Bullion (BYOB) Program
Any bullion meeting the purity, weight, denomination, and physical integrity requirements defined in this Act and the attached Schedule constitutes legal tender in the Hudson Republic, **regardless of origin, mint, producer, markings, or national symbol**. This includes existing coins, rounds, and bars circulating worldwide that satisfy the objective criteria.

---

## Section 4 — Verification Requirements
All bullion used as legal tender or held in reserves shall be subject to verification by XRF assay or equivalent non-destructive chemical analysis. Merchants and financial institutions must maintain records of verification for audit purposes under the Banking and Reserves Act.

---

## Section 5 — Prohibited Practices
No person or entity may debase, clip, shave, plate, or otherwise physically alter bullion in a manner that misrepresents its weight or purity. No person or entity shall create, issue, or circulate any paper, digital, or non-bullion instrument that purports to function as currency or credit of the Hudson Republic without express authorization. Violations constitute fraud and are punishable under the Criminal Code.
