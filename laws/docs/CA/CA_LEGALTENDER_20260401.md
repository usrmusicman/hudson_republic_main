# Legal Tender Act

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Bullion**: Physical metal in coin, round, or bar form composed of gold, silver, or copper meeting the purity and weight standards herein.  
- **Founders Edition**: The official reference series of Hudson Republic bullion coins minted to establish initial circulation standards, security features, and public recognition.  
- **XRF assay**: X-ray fluorescence or equivalent non-destructive chemical analysis used to verify elemental composition and purity.

---

## Preamble
This Act defines the standards for all bullion accepted as legal tender within the Hudson Republic. Legal tender status depends solely on verifiable physical properties—purity, weight, and integrity—not on origin, minting authority, or markings. The Act distinguishes between commercial and institutional use, mandates XRF or equivalent verification, and prohibits any unauthorized creation or imitation of currency instruments. All financial obligations, fees, fines, and settlements under this Act shall be discharged exclusively in physical bullion meeting these standards.

---

## Section 1 — Acceptable Bullion for Merchants (Commercial Use)
Bullion qualifies as legal tender for everyday commerce if it meets all of the following objective criteria:  
* Purity: Minimum 95% pure metal (gold, silver, or copper).  
* Weight: Between 1/8 troy ounce and 1 troy ounce, in exact 1/8 troy ounce increments.  
* Composition: Gold, silver, or copper, with approved hardeners as specified in the Bullion Standards Act.  
* Form: Single contiguous coin or round, free of dents, damage, clipping, shaving, or any physical alteration that misrepresents weight or purity.  

All normal commercial transactions shall assume 95% purity and shall not apply premiums or discounts for higher-purity metal.

---

## Section 2 — Acceptable Bullion for Financial Institutions
Bullion qualifies for use in banking, trusts, savings, and institutional reserves if it meets all of the following objective criteria:  
* Purity: Minimum 99.5% pure metal (gold, silver, or copper).  
* Weight: Minimum 1/8 troy ounce, in 1/8 troy ounce increments, with no upper limit.  
* Composition: Gold, silver, or copper, with approved hardeners as specified in the Bullion Standards Act.  
* Form: Single contiguous bar, free of dents, damage, clipping, shaving, or any physical alteration that misrepresents weight or purity.  

Bars may be fractionally cut or separated for institutional transactions only. All trusts and savings must be backed exclusively by bars meeting these standards; coins or rounds are not permitted for institutional backing.

---

## Section 3 — Bring Your Own Bullion (BYOB) Program
Any bullion meeting the purity, weight, and physical integrity requirements of Section 1 or Section 2 constitutes legal tender in the Hudson Republic, regardless of origin, mint, producer, mint mark, serial number, date, insignia, national symbol, or any other markings.

---

## Section 4 — Verification Requirements
All bullion used as legal tender or held in reserves shall be subject to verification by XRF assay or equivalent non-destructive chemical analysis to confirm purity and weight. Merchants and financial institutions must maintain records of verification for all significant transactions and are subject to audit under the Banking and Reserves Act.

---

## Section 5 — Prohibited Practices
No person or entity may debase, clip, shave, plate, or otherwise physically alter bullion in a manner that misrepresents its weight or purity. No person or entity shall create, issue, or circulate any paper, digital, or non-bullion instrument that purports to function as currency or credit of the Hudson Republic without express authorization. Violations constitute fraud and are punishable under the Criminal Code of the Hudson Republic.
