## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Riding**: A primary territorial and electoral division of the Hudson Republic, defined by land area rather than population, with each Riding approximating 35,000 to 50,000 square kilometers.  
- **Array**: A contiguous block of exactly twelve (12) Ridings, the minimum territorial unit for secession or full accession.  
- **Subzone**: A non-political administrative subdivision within a Riding used solely for logistics, postal, utility, emergency services, or internal mapping purposes.  
- **Satellite State**: A region that maintains a loose, non-voting affiliation with the Hudson Republic, as defined in this Act and cross-referenced in **[The Individual (Sovereign) Act](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**.

---

## Preamble
The Hudson Republic is constituted as a single, indivisible sovereign entity whose territory is organized into equal-area Ridings to ensure balanced representation, local accountability, and the rejection of population-weighted or hierarchical governance layers. Sovereignty resides with the People, expressed through direct Riding-based voting and strict constitutional limits on central power in accordance with **[The Individual (Sovereign) Act](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**. 

This Act establishes the territorial framework, Riding boundaries, redraw procedures, mechanisms for secession and accession, the management of Satellite States, and the absolute prohibition of intermediate provincial structures, thereby preserving modularity, equality, and the supremacy of the Individual’s rights over any administrative claim.

## Section 1 — The Republic and Territorial Basis
**1.1 Indivisible Sovereignty**: Sovereignty is indivisible and vests in the People as expressed through the Constitution.  
**1.2 Territorial Equality**: Each Riding elects exactly one Representative. Representation remains strictly one person, one vote. Riding boundaries are drawn to achieve approximately equal land area in accordance with the formula set out in Section 2.  
**1.3 Administrative Subzones**: Ridings may be divided into Subzones for logistics (postal, utility, emergency). Subzones hold **no** political or legislative authority.

---

## Section 2 — Definition and Establishment of Ridings
**2.1 The Grid Formula**: Riding boundaries are determined solely by landmass, not population. The size of each Riding is calculated as follows:  
Total Riding Size (km²) = Total Landmass of the Republic (km²) ÷ Total Number of Ridings.  

**2.2 Landmass Limits**: Each Riding shall encompass between **35,000 km² and 50,000 km²**, subject to the multiples rule below.  

**2.3 Tolerance for Irregular Boundaries**: To accommodate natural geographic features, sacred sites, military installations, or other federal assets that may span multiple Ridings, a tolerance of up to ±1% to ±5% of the calculated Riding size may be applied. The exact tolerance level shall be negotiated and agreed upon only when a redraw dispute is formally triggered by a 60% referendum within a block of adjacent Ridings and supported by a Senator, in accordance with the requirements of Section 3.  

**2.4 Multiples Rule**: The total number of Ridings must be a **multiple of twelve (12)**.  
**2.5 Capacity**: The total number of Ridings shall never be fewer than 12 or more than 144.  
**2.6 No Intermediate Layer**: There are **no provinces or states**. Governance moves directly from the Riding to the Federal level.

---

## Section 3 — Mapping and Periodicity of Redraw
**3.1 Ten-Year Lock**: Boundaries are reviewed only once every ten years.  
**3.2 Challenges**: Redraws require a Legislative Challenge (LC) and a referendum in the affected Senatorial block achieving **60% approval**, followed by a Senate clear verdict (7/12).

---

## Section 4 — Modification of the Union: Onboarding and Sunsetting

**4.1 Exit (Removal / Secession)**  
- **Unit Constraint**: Sovereignty is held at the Array level; exit requires a block of exactly twelve (12) contiguous Ridings.  
- **Scale Limits**: The seceding block must fall within the Republic’s standardized Array scale—minimum 420,000 km² to a maximum of 600,000 km².  
- **Popular Mandate**: Each constituent Riding within the Array must return a referendum result of no less than sixty percent (60%) approval for secession.  
- **Legislative Finality**: Requires a unanimous Senate verdict (12/12) across three readings.  
- **Choice of Status**: When filing for secession or exit, the applicant may choose to become a completely separate and independent entity or to maintain a looser relationship with the Hudson Republic as a Satellite State.

**4.2 Joining (Addition / Naturalization)**  
- **Physical Requirement**: Applying territories must satisfy the minimum landmass requirement (≥ 420,000 km²) and may not exceed the maximum threshold (≤ 600,000 km²) to form a new 12-Riding Array.  
- **Integrity Screening**: Applicants must undergo a full audit by the Hudson Intelligence Service (HIS).  
- **Ratification**: Joining requires a sixty percent (60%) popular referendum victory within the applying territory and a subsequent unanimous Senate verdict (12/12).

**4.3 Application Window**  
The application window for formal joining, adoption as Ridings, and secession is limited to **ninety (90) days** following the triggering of a territorial map redraw. Any application filed during this window must be processed together. Subsequent applications for the same redraw cycle will not be accepted; applicants must wait for the next ten-year redraw window or apply for Satellite State status instead.

---

## Section 5 — Satellite States (Limited Federation)
**5.1 Purpose and Status**  
Satellite States maintain a loose affiliation with the Hudson Republic. They receive trade access within the Hudson Economic Zone, military protection from the Hudson United Guard (HUG), and passport priority, but hold no voting rights in the Senate or participation in the National Judicial Hierarchy. All protections are limited to those expressly guaranteed by **[The Individual (Sovereign) Act](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**.

**5.2 Application Process**  
- An application fee of **fifty (50) troy ounces of gold** must be paid prior to any formal assessment. This fee must be paid exclusively from public funds raised by the applying state.  
- **Pre-Assessment**: The Republic shall calculate the impact of the proposed affiliation on existing Riding sizes.  
- **Landmass Requirement**: The applying region must encompass a **maximum** landmass of **thirty-five thousand (35,000) square kilometers**.  
- **Approval**: Two (2) Riding Representatives must sign the Satellite State partnership documents, and the National Representative must provide final sign-off.  
- Satellite State applications may be submitted **at any time**, as they do not form part of the Republic’s core territory.

**5.3 Mutual Exclusivity Rule**  
A region may apply for Satellite State status or for full adoption as Ridings, but not both simultaneously.

---

## Section 6 — Adoption of Satellite States as Ridings
**6.1 Eligibility for Adoption**  
A Satellite State may apply for full integration as one or more Ridings only after satisfying the following cumulative conditions (see **[CA_NATIONALSECURITY_20260401](./CA_NATIONALSECURITY_20260401.md)** for integrity screening).  

**6.2 Application Process**  
- An application fee of **five hundred (500) troy ounces of gold** must be paid prior to formal assessment.  
- **Pre-Assessment**: The total landmass of the applying region is added to the Republic’s existing territory. The resulting per-Riding landmass must remain within 35,000–50,000 km².  
- **Referendum and Senate Approval**: Adoption requires a Legislative Challenge (LC) and a referendum in the affected Senatorial block achieving **60% approval**, followed by a Senate clear verdict (7/12).  
- **National Representative Decision**: The National Representative may veto the adoption or approve it. Approval initiates a thirty (30)-year probationary period.

**6.3 Mutual Exclusivity Rule**  
A region that applies for one category and is rejected, rescinds, or is revoked must wait the full prescribed waiting period before applying for the other category.

---

## Section 7 — Claims Assessment Process
**7.1 General Principles**  
All applications for Satellite State status, adoption as Ridings, formal joining / naturalization, or secession shall undergo a formal Claims Assessment Process conducted by the Senate and the National Representative. Applications are processed first-come, first-served.

**7.2 Weighted Decision Factors**  
The assessment shall be based on the following weighted criteria:

- **First-Come, First-Served Priority (10–15%)**: Earlier applications receive greater consideration in cases of overlapping land claims.

- **Claim to Territory (70–75%)**: The assessment shall determine who holds the most recent valid title to the claimed territory, whether public or private.  
  - Valid claims require formal documentation (mortgages, deeds, financial statements, tax records, etc.).  
  - The claimant must demonstrate active utilization of the territory for industrial, residential, commercial, or other productive purposes.  
  - Any portion of the claimed territory that has had no title or ownership assigned in the past fifty (50) years at the date of application shall be deemed invalid for that claimant.  
  - In cases of overlapping counter-claims, the last substantiated claim shall prevail.  
  - If no valid claim can be substantiated, the territory defaults to the property of the Hudson Republic.

- **Miscellaneous Considerations (10–20%)**:  
  - If a validated claim fragments a larger region into non-contiguous or impractical subunits, affected territories shall negotiate to achieve the best contiguous fit.  
  - If continuity cannot be achieved due to intervening validated claims, the applicant may accept the adjusted territory or forfeit the application. This decision is final for the current application.

**7.3 Claim Hierarchy for Overlapping Applications**  
- Overlapping claims submitted for Satellite State status must be resolved by mutual agreement among the affected claimants. The Hudson Republic does not intervene in or adjudicate the internal pacts formed between claimants; it assesses such arrangements solely as international partnerships for administrative purposes under Satellite State status.  
- In cases involving a mix of claim types (e.g., a Satellite State application alongside a request for formal joining or adoption as Ridings), priority shall be given to the joining or “orphan” region. This hierarchy is designed to streamline the administrative onboarding of new territories as full Ridings within the Hudson Republic.

## Section 8 — The Senate
**8.1 Fixed Size**: The Senate consists of exactly **twelve (12) members** at all times.  
**8.2 Apportionment**: One Senator is elected per contiguous block of Ridings.

---

## Section 9 — The Modularity Principle
**9.1 Absolute Prohibition**: No provincial or state layer shall exist.  
**9.2 Riding Board**: Each Riding shall maintain a local Riding Board for municipal services, infrastructure, healthcare delivery, transportation, disaster relief, and other local matters. The **Riding Representative** serves as president of the Riding Board. The Board exercises full autonomy over local taxation (except federally prohibited income tax), budgets, tolls, fees, fines, and internal infrastructure. Direct inter-Riding transfers are prohibited; cooperation occurs only through Obligation Papers or Legislative Budgets.  
**9.3 Synchronization**: Local elections occur simultaneously with federal elections.

---

## Section 10 — Subzones and Administrative Allotments
**10.1** Subzones are for internal mapping and logistics only.  
**10.2** Subzones are strictly non-political.

---

## Section 11 — Property and Stakeholder Supremacy
**11.1** Real property is vested in the land of the Riding.  
**11.2 Rights Supremacy**: In any conflict between local bylaws and **[The Individual (Sovereign) Act](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**, the rights of the Individual prevail absolutely.
