# Territorial Provisioning and Domain Administration Act

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Riding**: A primary territorial and electoral division of the Hudson Republic, defined by land area rather than population, with each Riding approximating 35,000 to 50,000 square kilometers.  
- **Array**: A contiguous block of exactly twelve (12) Ridings, the minimum territorial unit for secession or full accession.  
- **Subzone**: A non-political administrative subdivision within a Riding used solely for logistics, postal, utility, emergency services, or internal mapping purposes.  
- **Satellite State**: A region that maintains a loose, non-voting affiliation with the Hudson Republic, as defined in this Act and cross-referenced in **[CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**.

---

## Preamble
The Hudson Republic is constituted as a single, indivisible sovereign entity whose territory is organized into equal-area Ridings to ensure balanced representation, local accountability, and the rejection of population-weighted or hierarchical governance layers. Sovereignty resides with the People, expressed through direct Riding-based voting and strict constitutional limits on central power. 

This Act defines the territorial framework, Riding establishment and redraw rules, mechanisms for secession and accession, the creation and management of Satellite States, the process for full adoption of Satellite States as Ridings, and the absolute prohibition of intermediate provincial structures, thereby preserving modularity, equality, and the supremacy of the Individual’s rights over any administrative claim.

---

## Section 1 — The Republic and Territorial Basis
**1.1 Indivisible Sovereignty**: Sovereignty is indivisible and vests in the People as expressed through the Constitution.  
**1.2 Territorial Equality**: Each Riding elects exactly one Representative. Representation remains strictly one person, one vote. Riding boundaries are drawn to achieve approximately equal land area in accordance with the formula set out in Section 2.  
**1.3 Administrative Subzones**: Ridings may be divided into Subzones for logistics (postal, utility, emergency). Subzones hold **no** political or legislative authority.

---

## Section 2 — Definition and Establishment of Ridings
**2.1 The Grid Formula**: Riding boundaries are determined solely by landmass, not population. The size of each Riding is calculated as follows:  
Total Riding Size (km²) = Total Landmass of the Republic (km²) ÷ Total Number of Ridings.  

**2.2 Landmass Limits**: Each Riding shall encompass between **35,000 km² and 50,000 km²**, subject to the multiples rule below.  

**2.3 Tolerance for Irregular Boundaries**: To accommodate natural geographic features, sacred sites, military installations, or other federal assets that may span multiple Ridings, a tolerance of up to ±1% to ±5% of the calculated Riding size may be applied. The exact tolerance level shall be negotiated and agreed upon only when a redraw dispute is formally triggered by a 60% referendum within a block of adjacent Ridings and supported by a Senator, in accordance with the requirements of Section 3.  

**2.4 Multiples Rule**: The total number of Ridings must be a **multiple of twelve (12)**.  
**2.5 Capacity**: The total number of Ridings shall never be fewer than 12 or more than 144.  
**2.6 No Intermediate Layer**: There are **no provinces or states**. Governance moves directly from the Riding to the Federal level.

---

## Section 3 — Mapping and Periodicity of Redraw
**3.1 Ten-Year Lock**: Boundaries are reviewed only once every ten years.  
**3.2 Challenges**: Redraws require a Legislative Challenge (LC) and a referendum in the affected Senatorial block achieving **60% approval**, followed by a Senate clear verdict (7/12).

---

## Section 4 — Modification of the Union: Onboarding and Sunsetting

**4.1 Exit (Removal / Secession)**  
- **Unit Constraint**: Sovereignty is held at the Array level; exit requires a block of exactly twelve (12) contiguous Ridings.  
- **Scale Limits**: The seceding block must fall within the Republic’s standardized Array scale—minimum 420,000 km² to a maximum of 600,000 km².  
- **Popular Mandate**: Each constituent Riding within the Array must return a referendum result of no less than sixty percent (60%) approval for secession.  
- **Legislative Finality**: Requires a unanimous Senate verdict (12/12) across three readings.

**4.2 Joining (Addition / Naturalization)**  
- **Physical Requirement**: Applying territories must satisfy the minimum landmass requirement (≥ 420,000 km²) and may not exceed the maximum threshold (≤ 600,000 km²) to form a new 12-Riding Array.  
- **Integrity Screening**: Applicants must undergo a full audit by the Hudson Intelligence Service (HIS).  
- **Ratification**: Joining requires a sixty percent (60%) popular referendum victory within the applying territory and a subsequent unanimous Senate verdict (12/12).

---

## Section 5 — Satellite States (Limited Federation)
**5.1 Purpose and Status**  
Satellite States maintain a loose affiliation with the Hudson Republic. They receive trade access within the Hudson Economic Zone, military protection from the Hudson United Guard (HUG), and passport priority, but hold no voting rights in the Senate or participation in the National Judicial Hierarchy. All protections are limited to those expressly guaranteed by **[The Individual (Sovereign) Act](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**.

**5.2 Application Process**  
- An application fee of one hundred (100) troy ounces of gold must be paid prior to any formal assessment. This fee must be paid exclusively from public funds raised by the applying state.  
- **Pre-Assessment**: The Republic shall calculate the impact of the proposed affiliation on existing Riding sizes.  
- **Approval**: Two (2) Riding Representatives must sign the Satellite State partnership documents, and the National Representative must provide final sign-off.

**5.3 Mutual Exclusivity Rule**  
A region may apply for Satellite State status or for full adoption as Ridings, but not both simultaneously.

**5.4 Fiscal Considerations**  
Prior to approval, the applying region must demonstrate a budgetary fiscal surplus for at least the preceding ten (10) years, a minimum credit rating of “A”, full conversion of all financial instruments into physical bullion meeting the standards of the **[Legal Tender Act](./CA_LEGALTENDER_20260401.md)**, and no receipt of direct transfers or subsidies from any external entity.

---

## Section 6 — Adoption of Satellite States as Ridings
**6.1 Eligibility for Adoption**  
A Satellite State may apply for full integration as one or more Ridings only after satisfying the following cumulative conditions (see Section 5.4 for fiscal requirements and **[CA_NATIONALSECURITY_20260401](./CA_NATIONALSECURITY_20260401.md)** for integrity screening).  

**6.2 Application Process**  
- An application fee of one thousand (1,000) troy ounces of gold must be paid prior to formal assessment.  
- **Pre-Assessment**: The total landmass of the applying region is added to the Republic’s existing territory. The resulting per-Riding landmass must remain within 35,000–50,000 km².  
- **Referendum and Senate Approval**: Adoption requires a Legislative Challenge (LC) and a referendum in the affected Senatorial block achieving **60% approval**, followed by a Senate clear verdict (7/12).  
- **National Representative Decision**: The National Representative may veto the adoption or approve it. Approval initiates a thirty (30)-year probationary period.

**6.3 Mutual Exclusivity Rule**  
A region that applies for one category and is rejected, rescinds, or is revoked must wait the full prescribed waiting period before applying for the other category.

---

## Section 7 — The Senate
**7.1 Fixed Size**: The Senate consists of exactly **twelve (12) members** at all times.  
**7.2 Apportionment**: One Senator is elected per contiguous block of Ridings.

---

## Section 8 — The Modularity Principle
**8.1 Absolute Prohibition**: No provincial or state layer shall exist.  
**8.2 Riding Board**: Each Riding shall maintain a local Riding Board for municipal services, infrastructure, healthcare delivery, transportation, disaster relief, and other local matters. The **Riding Representative** serves as president of the Riding Board. The Board exercises full autonomy over local taxation (except federally prohibited income tax), budgets, tolls, fees, fines, and internal infrastructure. Direct inter-Riding transfers are prohibited; cooperation occurs only through Obligation Papers or Legislative Budgets.  
**8.3 Synchronization**: Local elections occur simultaneously with federal elections.

---

## Section 9 — Subzones and Administrative Allotments
**9.1** Subzones are for internal mapping and logistics only.  
**9.2** Subzones are strictly non-political.

---

## Section 10 — Property and Stakeholder Supremacy
**10.1** Real property is vested in the land of the Riding.  
**10.2 Rights Supremacy**: In any conflict between local bylaws and **[The Individual (Sovereign) Act](./CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401.md)**, the rights of the Individual prevail absolutely.
