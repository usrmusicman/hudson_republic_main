## Definitions

- **Core Rights**: The fundamental liberties enumerated in Sections 2 through 5 of this Act.
- **Strict Scrutiny**: The highest standard of judicial review. Any limitation on Core Rights must be narrowly tailored to serve a compelling governmental interest and must be the least restrictive means available.
- **Good Faith Official Action**: Conduct undertaken with lawful authority, based on reasonable evidence, and without malice or reckless disregard for individual rights.
- **Sequential Mandate**: Cumulative sentencing structure in which penalties for separate violations are served consecutively.

## Preamble

The rights and liberties of the Individual are the supreme foundation of the Hudson Republic. Government exists solely as a fiduciary servant of the People and derives all legitimate authority from the protection and preservation of these rights. These rights are inherent, pre-political, and pre-exist the State. No authority may abrogate them. Limitations on their exercise are permitted only under the strictest judicial standards as defined in this Act.

---

## Section 1 — Supremacy of Rights

**1.1** The Core Rights enumerated in this Act are the supreme law of the Hudson Republic and bind all branches and levels of government.  
**1.2** No law, decree, treaty, or emergency measure may abrogate Core Rights. Any such act is void to the extent of the inconsistency.  
**1.3** Limitations on Core Rights are permissible only when they satisfy the Strict Scrutiny Standard.  
**1.4** Violations of this Act by public officials trigger personal accountability under law.  
**1.5** Emergency powers may impose temporary, narrowly tailored limitations consistent with Section 8, but may never suspend judicial review or eliminate Core Rights.

---

## Section 2 — Fundamental Liberties

**2.1** Every person possesses the rights to freedom of conscience, thought, expression, press, peaceful assembly, association, and petition.  
**2.2** These freedoms may be limited only to prevent direct, imminent, and demonstrable harm to the rights of others, subject to Strict Scrutiny.  
**2.3** Every individual has absolute self-ownership, including the rights to life, bodily integrity, liberty of movement, and personal autonomy.  
**2.4** Bodily autonomy shall not be infringed except under Strict Scrutiny where necessary to prevent substantial harm to others.

---

## Section 3 — Property and Economic Liberty

**3.1** Every person has the right to acquire, possess, use, enjoy, and dispose of property.  
**3.2** Property may be taken only for genuine public necessity, with prior due process and just compensation at full fair market value.  
**3.3** Compensation must be paid in a stable medium that does not diminish the owner’s purchasing power.  
**3.4** Regulation shall not render property effectively unusable without compensation.  
**3.5** Taxation must be lawful, transparent, and non-confiscatory.

---

## Section 4 — Defense of Life and Liberty

**4.1** The right to self-defense and defense of one’s family and property is inherent and pre-exists government.  
**4.2** Citizens may keep and bear arms suitable for lawful self-defense.  
**4.3** Any regulation of this right must be narrowly tailored to genuine public safety concerns and must preserve the practical ability of self-defense.

---

## Section 5 — Justice and Due Process

**5.1** No excessive fines or disproportionate punishments shall be imposed.  
**5.2** All persons are secure against unreasonable searches and seizures. Warrants require probable cause and particularity.  
**5.3** The accused is entitled to a speedy and public trial, an impartial tribunal, counsel, confrontation of witnesses, and compulsory process for favorable witnesses.

---

## Section 6 — Civic Participation

**6.1** Participation in civic life is both a right and a voluntary duty.  
**6.2** No person shall be compelled into service as a condition of exercising their rights.  
**6.3** Constitutional literacy is encouraged for all citizens and may be required for naturalization through fair and accessible standards.

---

## Section 7 — Integrity of Public Office

**7.1** Public office is a fiduciary duty owed to the People.  
**7.2** Officials bear personal accountability for unlawful acts.  
**7.3** Good Faith Official Action shall not incur personal liability unless the violation was knowing, reckless, or grossly negligent.  
**7.4** Corruption, bribery, or abuse of power shall result in removal from office and prosecution.

---

## Section 8 — Emergency Powers

**8.1** Emergency measures may only be enacted in response to a clear, evidence-based threat to life, public safety, or the survival of the Republic.  
**8.2** All emergency measures must satisfy the Strict Scrutiny Standard, be time-limited, publicly justified, and immediately reviewable by the judiciary.  
**8.3** Emergency actions automatically expire unless affirmatively renewed under the same standards.  
**8.4** Emergency powers may never eliminate judicial review or permanently suspend Core Rights.

---

## Section 9 — Enforcement and Remedies

**9.1** Any person may challenge a violation of this Act before the courts.  
**9.2** Courts shall grant appropriate relief including injunctions, restitution, and penalties against responsible officials where warranted.  
**9.3** The burden of justification rests upon the State in all cases involving limitations on Core Rights.

---

## Section 10 — Interpretation

**10.1** This Act shall be interpreted liberally in favor of individual liberty and strictly against the expansion of government power.  
**10.2** Where rights appear to conflict, courts shall seek to maximize the liberty of all parties through the Strict Scrutiny Standard.
