# The Individual (Sovereign) Act

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Sequential Mandate**: Cumulative sentencing for violations of fiduciary duty, treason, or sedition, with penalties served consecutively.

---

## Preamble
The rights, liberties, and duties of the Individual are the supreme foundation of the Hudson Republic. Government exists solely as a fiduciary servant of the People, bound at all times to protect and never infringe these immutable protections. No emergency, decree, treaty, or legislative act may suspend, diminish, or override them except through the rigorous constitutional amendment process. Any contrary action is void *ab initio* and constitutes a grave breach of fiduciary duty, punishable as treason or sedition. These rights are granted to all citizens of the Republic at the point of conception.

---

## Section 1 — Supremacy of Rights

**1.1** The rights and liberties enumerated herein are supreme law and immutable.  
**1.2** No amendment to these rights shall occur except through the unanimous Senate process (12 out of 12 seated senators on each of three readings) as defined in the Legislative Framework Act.  
**1.3** Any official act, decree, or emergency measure that infringes these rights without following the amendment procedure is legally null and void.  
**1.4** Perpetrators of such infringement shall face prosecution for treason or sedition, with personal liability triggered and no immunity available.  
**1.5** Emergency powers shall never suspend or diminish these rights. All emergency actions remain subject to immediate public audit and review by the Supreme Court (see Judicial Courts Act).

---

## Section 2 — Fundamental Liberties

**2.1** Every person within the Republic enjoys absolute freedom of conscience, thought, speech, press, assembly, petition, and association.  
**2.2** No law, bylaw, or official compulsion may restrict these freedoms except to prevent direct, imminent, and specific harm to another person’s rights.  
**2.3 Self-ownership** is inviolable. Every person possesses the right to life, bodily integrity, liberty of movement, and the pursuit of personal well-being. Bodily autonomy is the primary and inalienable property of the individual.

---

## Section 3 — Property and the Stakeholder Principle

**3.1** Every person has the absolute right to acquire, possess, use, enjoy, and transfer property.  
**3.2** Property ownership constitutes a stake in the Republic and is essential to individual sovereignty.  
**3.3** Property may be taken only for essential national infrastructure projects of clear public necessity, and only with prior just compensation at full market value in physical bullion.  
**3.4** No taxation, regulation, or seizure may be imposed that effectively confiscates or renders property unusable without due process and compensation.

---

## Section 4 — Defense of Life and Liberty

**4.1** The inherent right to defend one’s own life, family, person, and property is inviolable and pre-exists government.  
**4.2** Citizens possess the right to keep and bear armaments suitable for lawful self-defense, family protection, and community security.  
**4.3** Any regulation of this right must be narrowly tailored to genuine public safety concerns, consistent with national defense needs, and may not infringe the core purpose of self-defense.

---

## Section 5 — Justice and Security

**5.1** No excessive fines, cruel or unusual punishments, or disproportionate sanctions shall be imposed. All penalties must be strictly proportionate to the offense.  
**5.2** Every person is secure in their person, house, papers, and effects against unreasonable search and seizure. No warrant shall issue without probable cause, supported by oath or affirmation, particularly describing the place to be searched and persons or things to be seized. All warrants and searches are subject to judicial audit.  
**5.3** In all criminal prosecutions, the accused is entitled to a speedy and public trial by an impartial jury, to be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation, to confront witnesses, to compel favorable witnesses, and to the assistance of counsel. These protections apply to all persons under Republic jurisdiction, regardless of location.

---

## Section 6 — Civic Duty and Participation

**6.1** Citizens possess both the right and the duty to participate voluntarily in the life of the Republic through service in infrastructure, civic administration, defense, or community projects.  
**6.2** All participation in state-sponsored programs remains strictly voluntary. No service may be compelled as a condition of citizenship or rights retention.  
**6.3 Constitutional literacy**—understanding of the founding principles, rights, and structure of the Republic—is a mandatory requirement for naturalization and strongly encouraged for all citizens.

---

## Section 7 — Integrity and Public Ethos

**7.1** All public officials serve as Independents and bear absolute personal liability for their actions. Corruption, bribery, fraud, or acceptance of undue influence from external entities or prohibited political organizations constitutes a material breach of the Oath of Integrity.  
**7.2** Any such breach triggers immediate termination, revocation of office, and prosecution under the Sequential Mandate with cumulative penalties up to life imprisonment.  
**7.3** All treaties, international contracts, and agreements are binding only after full constitutional review and may never override the rights of the Individual or the territorial sovereignty of the Ridings.
