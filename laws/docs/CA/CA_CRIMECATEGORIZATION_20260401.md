# Crime Categorization Act

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Misdemeanor**: A reformable criminal act or nuisance where the primary goals are deterrence and behavioral reformation before escalation to a felony. Misdemeanors may be expunged by a qualified judge upon demonstrated reformation.  
- **Felony**: A grave offense against the safety, well-being, or institutions of the Republic. The primary goals are punishment, deterrence, and protection of society. Felonies are permanent unless formally overturned on appeal.  
- **Capital Offense**: A direct attack on the Republic’s sovereignty, the stability of its people, or its institutions. These offenses carry the most severe penalties, including natural life or death.  
- **Life Term**: Two generations (30 years cumulative) or the longest political term ever served by any public official.  
- **Natural Life**: Imprisonment for the remainder of the offender’s natural life with no possibility of parole unless the conviction is overturned on appeal.  
- **Judgment Redemption Note (JRN)**: A formal judicial note detailing an offender’s reformation progress, signed by the judge, witnesses, and the offender for full auditability.  
- **Sequential Mandate**: Cumulative sentencing for repeated or escalating violations, served consecutively.

---

## Preamble
This Act establishes a clear, tiered classification system for criminal offenses in the Hudson Republic. It defines misdemeanors, felonies, and capital offenses with precise sentencing guidelines, expungement rules, and appeal pathways. The system prioritizes reformation for lesser offenses while imposing permanent and severe consequences for grave crimes against the Republic and its people. All classifications operate in full compliance with **The Individual (Sovereign) Act** and the **Judicial Courts Act**.

## Section 1 — Criminal Classification Levels (0–9)

**Level 0** — No conviction or overturned: No record exists. Either the conviction was overturned or a not-guilty verdict was rendered.

**Level 1 — Small Infractions**  
Minor violations leading to small fines or restitution (up to 1/8 troy ounce gold cumulative). Expunged immediately upon payment.  

**Level 2 — Larger Infractions**  
Violations leading to moderate fines or restitution (up to 1 troy ounce gold cumulative) and/or community service. Expunged upon full compliance.  

**Level 3 — Lower Criminal Grade Offense (Misdemeanor)**  
Offenses punishable by fines, restitution, community service, and/or prison time (up to 1 year cumulative). May be expunged upon judicial judgment of reformation.  

**Level 4 — Low-Grade Felony**  
Punishable by fines, restitution, community service, and/or prison time (up to 3 years cumulative). Permanent unless overturned on appeal. A Judgment Redemption Note may be issued upon release.  

**Level 5 — Harmful Felony**  
Punishable by fines, restitution, and/or prison time (up to 5 years cumulative). Permanent unless overturned. Judgment Redemption Note may be issued.  

**Level 6 — Severe Felony**  
Punishable by fines, restitution, and/or prison time (up to 15 years cumulative). Permanent unless overturned. Judgment Redemption Note may be issued.  

**Level 7 — Supreme Felony**  
Punishable by fines, restitution, and/or prison time (up to 30 years cumulative). Permanent unless overturned. Judgment Redemption Note may be issued.  

**Level 8 — Capital High Crime**  
Mandatory natural life sentence. Permanent unless overturned. No redemption process.  

**Level 9 — Absolute Conviction**  
Mandatory death sentence. Permanent unless overturned. No redemption process.

## Section EX1 - Criminal Classification Levels (examples only)

**Level 1 — Small Infractions**  
Examples: jaywalking, parking violations, minor traffic infractions.

**Level 2 — Larger Infractions**  
Examples: petty theft (under 1/8 troy ounce gold), defacing property, public nuisance without harm.

**Level 3 — Lower Criminal Grade Offense (Misdemeanor)**   
Examples: minor assault (e.g., drunken scuffle), misdemeanor theft (under 1 troy ounce gold), property damage (under 1 troy ounce gold).

**Level 4 — Low-Grade Felony**  
Examples: grand theft (over 1 troy ounce gold), impersonating a peace officer, transporting contraband across Riding lines.

**Level 5 — Harmful Felony**  
Examples: assault causing moderate bodily harm without a weapon, resisting arrest with violence, child endangerment.

**Level 6 — Severe Felony**   
Examples: assault with a deadly weapon causing life-changing injury, distribution of child exploitation material, first-offense sedition, kidnapping.

**Level 7 — Supreme Felony**  
Examples: second-offense sedition, sexual assault of an adult, attempted murder, racketeering, drug trafficking without deaths.

**Level 8 — Capital High Crime**  
Examples: third-offense sedition, voluntary manslaughter, serial sexual assault of adults, terrorism.

**Level 9 — Absolute Conviction**  
Examples: treason, terrorism causing mass casualties, aggravated murder, serial sexual assault of minors, drug trafficking causing death.
