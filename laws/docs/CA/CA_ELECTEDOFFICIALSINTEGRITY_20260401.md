# Elected Officials and Integrity Act

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Strict Blind Trust**: A trust in which the official retains no control, knowledge, or influence over the management or disposition of assets, managed by an independent trustee approved by the Judiciary Audit Office.  
- **Sequential Mandate**: Cumulative sentencing for violations of fiduciary duty, abuse of power, or sedition, with penalties served consecutively.  
- **The Individual (Sovereign) Act**: Refers to **CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401**.  
- **Legislative Challenge (LC)**: A formal challenge to legislation or budgets under the rules established in the Legislative Framework Act.

---

## Preamble
Public office in the Hudson Republic is a fiduciary trust, not a career or privilege. Elected officials serve as temporary stewards of sovereignty, bound by strict eligibility requirements, separation from private interests, non-delegation of authority, and lifelong personal accountability to prevent capture, corruption, or abuse. All roles carry high barriers to entry, rigid term and career limits, upward-only mobility, mandatory retirement, and severe sequential penalties for breaches. Integrity mechanisms—including blind trusts, biannual reviews, and automatic by-elections—ensure officials remain decoupled from external influence and faithful to the people and the Constitution.

---

## General Eligibility Requirements for All Public Office
All candidates for elected office (Riding Representative, Senator, HoC Mediator, Senate Mediator, National Representative, Supreme Court Justice) must meet the following universal criteria at the time of candidacy and throughout their term:  

* **Sole Citizenship**: Exclusive citizenship in the Hudson Republic; no dual or multiple citizenships.  
* **Riding Residency**: Continuous legal residency in the specific Riding for at least five (5) full years immediately preceding election (waived for national roles where not applicable).  
* **Age**: Between twenty-five (25) and sixty-five (65) years at the time of election.  
* **Financial Decoupling**: Within thirty (30) days of election, officials must forfeit all private executive or board titles and place all controlled assets into a **Strict Blind Trust**.  
* **No Party Affiliation**: Candidates must run without any party affiliation, endorsement, or organizational support. No registered political party or faction may fund or coordinate with any candidate.  
  * Candidates appear on the ballot solely under their legally authorized personal name.  
  * Any evidence of party influence results in immediate disqualification and potential prosecution for election interference.

## Section 1 — Riding Representative

**1.1 Duties**: The elected fiduciary agent of their Riding; responsible for proposing and providing fiscal oversight on primary legislation.

### Tenure and Career Limits
**1.2** Terms are five (5) years. The maximum aggregate career limit across all elected roles is fifteen (15) years.  
**1.3 Lateral and Downward Lock**: Ineligible if they previously held higher office (Senator, Mediator, Justice, or National Representative). May only move upward to National Representative.  
**1.4 Mandatory Retirement**: Must retire from all political roles upon reaching the 15-year cap unless transitioning to the Executive Office.

### Administrative Personnel and Sedition
**1.5** Staff may be appointed solely for operational support.  
**1.6 Non-Delegation Doctrine**: Legislative authority is held exclusively by the elected official; no transfer of legislative intent is permitted.  
**1.7 Sedition**: Any performance of legislative acts by non-elected staff constitutes sedition.

### Criminal Liability
**1.8 Sequential Mandate**: Convictions for abuse of power or sedition are subject to cumulative sequential sentencing (served consecutively).  
**1.9 Penalties**: May include up to life imprisonment per count.

---

## Section 2 — HoC Mediator

**2.1 Election**: Elected by a sixty percent (60%) vote of all sitting Riding Representatives, ninety (90) days after each general election.  
**2.2 Duties**: Neutral procedural officer of the House of Constituents. At every reading and at the final rendering stage, the HoC Mediator’s sole duty is to receive legislation exactly as passed and transmit it, unchanged, to the next stage or to the Senate.  
**2.3 Neutrality**: The HoC Mediator shall have **zero influence** over the content, timing, or outcome of any legislation. Any attempt to influence, delay, or alter legislation constitutes treason under CO_TREASON_20260401.  
**2.4 Responsibilities and Consequences**: Identical to those of the Senate Mediator and the volunteer Supreme Court Justice (see Sections 3 and 6).

---

## Section 3 — Senator

**3.1 Duties**: Maintains constitutional stability as fiduciary for their assigned Riding Block.  
**3.2 Authority**: Veto via 7/12 clear verdict; initiate “Recall for Audit” on legislation.  
**3.3 Non-Author Status**: Strictly prohibited from drafting or co-sponsoring peacetime legislation.

### Tenure and Career Limits
**3.4** Five-year terms; 15-year aggregate career cap.  
**3.5 Vertical Integrity Lock**: Ineligible if previously served as Mediator, Representative, Justice, or National Representative.

### Individual Emergency Protocols and Liability
**3.6 Unlimited Personal Liability**: Triggered immediately for any affirmative vote on emergency extension or Declaration of War.  
**3.7 Forfeiture of Immunity**: Affirmative voters waive all immunities and are personally and financially liable for overreach.  
**3.8 Lifelong Accountability**: No statute of limitations for acts under extraordinary mandate.

---

## Section 4 — Senate Mediator

**4.1 Duties**: Neutral procedural officer of the Senate. At every reading and at the final rendering stage, the Senate Mediator’s sole duty is to receive legislation exactly as passed and transmit it, unchanged, to the next stage or to the Executive Office for final review and minting.  
**4.2 Neutrality**: The Senate Mediator shall have **zero influence** over the content, timing, or outcome of any legislation. Any attempt to influence, delay, or alter legislation constitutes treason under CO_TREASON_20260401.  
**4.3 Responsibilities and Consequences**: Identical to those of the HoC Mediator and the volunteer Supreme Court Justice.

### Tenure and Eligibility
**4.4** Five-year terms; 15-year aggregate cap (or 30 years total political service).  
**4.5 Lock**: Ineligible if previously held Senator, Representative, Justice, or National Representative roles.

### Powers of Order
**4.6** May call mandatory “Cooling” Recess.  
**4.7** Sole arbiter of Senate conduct and professionalism.

---

## Section 5 — National Representative

**5.1 Duties**: Protects Republic sovereignty; final arbiter for national disputes.  
**5.2 Tenure**: Maximum three five-year terms (15 years total).  
**5.3 Experience Requirement**: Must have served at least one full term as Riding Representative, Senator, or Senate Mediator.

### Criminal Liability and Sentencing
**5.4 Sequential Mandate**: Cumulative sentencing for Abuse of Power, Rights Violations, or Sedition.  
**5.5 Calculation**: Each infringed right or day of unconstitutional order is a separate count.  
**5.6 Emergency Liability**: Unlimited personal and financial liability for overreach triggered immediately upon Proclamation of Emergency.

---

## Section 6 — Supreme Court Justices

**6.1 Election**: Six Justices are elected by the Senate by clear verdict (7/12) immediately following each general election.  
**6.2 Term Limits**: Maximum of three five-year terms (15 years total), aligned with all other elected roles.  
**6.3 Jurisdiction**: Exclusive jurisdiction over matters of constitutionality.  
**6.4 Constraints**: Do not handle standard criminal or civil law and do not impose sentences.  
**6.5 Accountability**: Justices must maintain a record of professionalism and rigor. Failure to do so triggers bi-annual integrity review or Legislative Challenge.  

**6.6 Legislative Challenges (LC)**: Only one sitting Justice may volunteer to submit Legislative Challenges. At the final rendering stage, the volunteer Justice’s sole role is to transmit the LC, unchanged, to the Senate. The Justice shall have **zero influence** over the content or outcome beyond their single vote as a Justice. Any attempt to influence or alter an LC constitutes treason under CO_TREASON_20260401.  
**6.7 Responsibilities and Consequences**: Identical to those of the HoC Mediator and Senate Mediator.

---

## Section 7 — Common Rules for Mediators and Volunteer Justice
The HoC Mediator, Senate Mediator, and volunteer Supreme Court Justice for Legislative Challenges share identical duties, responsibilities, and consequences when acting in their rendering capacity:  
- Strict neutrality and **zero influence** over legislative content, timing, or outcome at every reading level and at the final stage.  
- Sole function is to receive and transmit decisions exactly as passed.  
- Any breach constitutes treason under CO_TREASON_20260401 and triggers the Sequential Mandate.

---

## Section 8 — Emergency Appointments

**8.1 Definition**: Interim appointments to fill vacancies until an election is completed.  
**8.2 Procedure**:  
* Requires a signed waiver of office (or MD certification of death/incapacity).  
* National Representative appoints interim custodial replacements (no new policy or legislation permitted).  
* If the National Representative is incapacitated, the Senate assumes the highest office, with the Mediator acting as **Interim Custodial Senator**.  
* **Military Custody**: If all legislatures fail, the Armed Forces may assume temporary custodial authority *solely* to maintain essential services and facilitate elections.

---

## Section 9 — Non-Delegation of Intent

**9.1 Permitted Assistance**: Staff and AI may be used for research and preliminary drafting only.  
**9.2 Absolute Personal Responsibility**: The final legislative text must be the product of the official’s personal review. Delegation of substantive intent is strictly prohibited.  
**9.3 Omnibus Legislation Prohibition**: Bundling unrelated subjects into one instrument is a high misdemeanor.  
**9.4 Plain Language Requirement**: All legislation must be drafted in clear, plain language comprehensible to the average citizen. Legalese designed to obscure meaning is prohibited.
