# Military and Defence Act

---

## Definitions
All capitalized terms used in this Act shall be interpreted in accordance with their definitions in the referenced instruments below.

- **Hudson United Guard (HUG)**: The sole national defence force of the Hudson Republic.  
- **Sequential Mandate**: Cumulative sentencing for violations of fiduciary duty, treason, or sedition, with penalties served consecutively.  
- **The Individual Act**: Refers to **CA_THEINDIVIDUAL_20260401** (The Individual (Sovereign) Act).  
- **CA class**: Constitutional Articles requiring unanimous Senate verdict (12/12) on three readings for amendment.

---

## Preamble
The defence of the Hudson Republic is an essential federal responsibility, exercised solely to protect the indivisible sovereignty of the Republic, the immutable rights of the Individual, and the territorial integrity of its Ridings. The **Hudson United Guard (HUG)** exists only as an instrument of last resort, subordinate to the Constitution, strictly limited in purpose and funding, and subject to personal accountability for any abuse. No military force shall ever be used to advance private interests, suppress constitutional rights, or project power beyond the Republic’s borders except in direct response to an armed attack or imminent existential threat.

---

## Section 1 — The Hudson United Guard (HUG)
**1.1** The HUG is the sole national defence force of the Republic.  

**1.2 Core Responsibilities**:  
* Protection from external armed threats and internal armed insurrection.  
* Deployment in support of declared emergencies or authorized rescue missions.  
* General security, border control, and patrols only *after* a formal declaration of war is enacted.  

**1.3 Strict Prohibitions**: The HUG shall **never** be used to:  
* Act on behalf of any private interest.  
* Deprive any citizen or Riding of rights under **The Individual Act**.  
* Conduct foreign missions without a direct armed attack on the Republic.  
* **Penalty**: Violations constitute treason or sedition, triggering the **Sequential Mandate** (cumulative sentencing).

---

## Section 2 — Military Funding
**2.1 Wartime Funding**: Budget shall not exceed the annual reserve ratio of total bullion holdings in the **National Holdings Vault**.  
**2.2 Peacetime Funding**: Budget is capped at **fifty percent (50%)** of the wartime reserve ratio.  
**2.3 Approval**: All military budgets are **LB class** (two readings in the HoC, two in the Senate). No off-budget expenditures are permitted.

---

## Section 3 — Declaration of War
**3.1 Initiation**: Issued only by the National Representative; must be transmitted to the Senate within 24 hours.  
**3.2 Validation**: Requires **monthly unanimous validation (12/12)** by the Senate.  
**3.3 Termination**: Failure to achieve a 12/12 vote in any given month immediately terminates the state of war and all associated funding.

---

## Section 4 — Accountability
**4.1 Personal Liability**: Every member of the HUG—from leadership to servicemen and servicewomen—bears personal liability for actions violating the Constitution.  
**4.2 Audit**: All operations and expenditures are subject to immediate public audit and **Supreme Court** review upon request.

---

## Section 5 — Military Leadership and Command Structure
**5.1 Clearances**:  
* Leadership: Top Clearance.  
* Servicemen and servicewomen: Enhanced Clearance.  

**5.2 Commander-in-Chief**: The National Representative is the civilian head; orders violating constitutional rights are unlawful on their face.  

**5.3 National General (Federal)**:  
* Highest military authority during war or emergency.  
* **Veto Power**: Explicit authority to stand down and refuse orders from the National Representative that violate the Constitution (**CA class**).  

**5.4 Regional General (Riding/Block)**:  
* Highest authority within a Riding or Riding Block.  
* Authority to refuse unlawful orders from the National General.  

**5.5 Site General (Base/Camp)**:  
* Commands specific operational sites.  
* Authority to refuse unlawful orders from the Regional General.  

**5.6 Lead Serviceman / Servicewoman (Team Lead)**:  
* Responsible for specific missions and reservist team readiness.

---

## Section 6 — Constitutional Safeguards and Stand-Down Authority
**6.1 Mandatory Refusal**: Every member of the HUG has the explicit **duty** to refuse any order that violates the Constitution or **The Individual Act**.  
**6.2 Protected Conduct**: Refusal of an unlawful order cannot be grounds for discipline or prosecution.  
**6.3 Anti-Retaliation**: Any attempt to punish a member for exercising stand-down authority constitutes **sedition or treason**.
