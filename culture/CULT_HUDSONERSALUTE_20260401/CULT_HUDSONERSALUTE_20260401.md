# The Hudsoner Salute

**A Founding Poem of the Hudson Republic**

---

## The Salute

We believe in the majesty of this glorious land,  
whose body protects us and whose waters quench our thirst.  

She is the fruitful provider, with boundless vineyards, orchards, and plains.  
Her splendid beauty is on display for all,  
from her meadows, to her forests, to her rivers and ports at sea.  

She bears great and industrious people,  
who harvests and builds her,  
from the plains of Manitoba to the oceans of Labrador.  

We will protect her in labor, and rejoice in her new beginnings.  
The fine land on which we stand shall be set forth for future generations.  

May we embrace her beauty, in great jubilant song, and in the horizon.  
We stand at her side, tall, strong, and free.  

Embraced by graceful majesty,  
empowered by duty,  
and emboldened by ambition,  
like the mighty beaver.  

We are the mighty Hudsoners, strong and built by the Creator.  
The Hudson flows within you, and within me.  
We are Hudsoners; we are free.
